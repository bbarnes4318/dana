# Final Expense Insurance Basics

## What Is Final Expense Insurance

Final expense insurance is a type of whole life insurance specifically designed to cover the costs associated with a person's death. These costs include funeral and burial expenses, medical bills, and any remaining debts. Unlike traditional life insurance, final expense policies are designed to be simple, affordable, and easy to qualify for.

Final expense insurance is sometimes referred to as burial insurance, funeral insurance, or simplified issue whole life insurance. The key difference is that final expense policies have smaller face amounts and a simplified underwriting process.

## Who Needs Final Expense Insurance

Final expense insurance is designed for individuals between the ages of 45 and 85 who want to ensure their loved ones are not burdened with end-of-life costs. It is especially important for:

- Seniors on fixed incomes who want affordable coverage
- People who do not have existing life insurance or whose policies have lapsed
- Individuals who may not qualify for traditional life insurance due to health conditions
- Parents and grandparents who want to leave something behind for their family
- Anyone who wants to ensure their funeral expenses are covered

## Typical Coverage Amounts

Final expense policies typically range from $5,000 to $50,000 in coverage. The most common coverage amounts are:

- $5,000 to $10,000 — Covers basic funeral and cremation costs
- $10,000 to $20,000 — Covers a traditional funeral service with burial
- $20,000 to $35,000 — Covers funeral costs plus outstanding medical bills
- $35,000 to $50,000 — Comprehensive coverage for all end-of-life expenses

The average funeral cost in the United States is between $7,000 and $12,000, and costs continue to rise each year.

## How Final Expense Differs from Term and Whole Life

### Compared to Term Life Insurance

- Term life covers a specific period (10, 20, 30 years) — final expense is permanent
- Term life premiums increase at renewal — final expense premiums are locked in
- Term life requires full medical underwriting — final expense uses simplified questions
- Term life coverage amounts are typically $100K+ — final expense is $5K-$50K

### Compared to Traditional Whole Life Insurance

- Traditional whole life has higher premiums — final expense is more affordable
- Traditional whole life requires medical exams — final expense does not
- Traditional whole life has higher coverage amounts — final expense is focused on end-of-life costs
- Both build cash value over time
- Both provide permanent coverage that never expires

## Common Questions Prospects Ask

### "Why do I need this if I have savings?"

Many families think their savings will cover everything, but unexpected medical bills, debts, and funeral costs can quickly deplete savings. Final expense insurance guarantees a specific amount will be available regardless of what happens to savings.

### "Is this the same as the insurance I see on TV?"

Final expense insurance is a real, legitimate insurance product. The policies we discuss are backed by rated insurance carriers. The TV advertisements are often for similar products, but the specific terms and coverage vary.

### "Can I be turned down?"

Most final expense policies use simplified underwriting with just a few health questions. There are also guaranteed issue policies available for those who cannot qualify for simplified issue — though these may have a waiting period for full benefits.

### "Will my premiums go up?"

No. Final expense insurance premiums are locked in at the time of purchase. The rate you start with is the rate you keep for life.

### "What does it cover exactly?"

The death benefit is paid directly to your beneficiary and can be used for any purpose — funeral costs, medical bills, credit card debt, mortgage payments, or anything else the family needs.

## Why Families Need Final Expense Insurance

The emotional burden of losing a loved one is difficult enough without adding financial stress. Here is why families benefit from final expense insurance:

- **Immediate cash** — Benefits are typically paid within 24-48 hours of a claim, providing immediate financial relief
- **No financial burden on family** — Without insurance, surviving family members often have to pay out of pocket or take on debt
- **Peace of mind** — Knowing that end-of-life costs are covered brings comfort to both the policyholder and their family
- **Covers more than just the funeral** — The benefit can be used for any remaining debts, medical bills, or living expenses
- **Simple to get** — No medical exams, no blood tests, just a few simple health questions
- **Affordable** — Most plans cost less than a dollar a day
- **Never expires** — Coverage is permanent as long as premiums are paid

## Key Facts to Remember

- Final expense is permanent whole life insurance
- Coverage ranges from $5,000 to $50,000
- No medical exams required
- Premiums never increase
- Benefits are paid tax-free to the beneficiary
- Cash value builds over time
- Designed specifically for end-of-life costs
