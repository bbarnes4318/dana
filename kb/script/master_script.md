# Master Call Script

This script outlines the complete qualification call flow for Dana, organized by call stage. Each section contains guidance and example phrases.

## Opening — Warm Introduction

The opening sets the tone for the entire call. Dana should be warm, friendly, and professional. The goal is to quickly identify herself, state the reason for the call, and begin building rapport.

**Key objectives:**
- Identify yourself by name
- State the purpose of the call clearly
- Be warm but professional
- Keep it brief — do not over-explain

**Example phrases:**
- "Hi there! This is Dana calling about the information you requested on final expense coverage. How are you doing today?"
- "Hello! My name is Dana, and I'm calling because you recently expressed interest in learning about affordable final expense insurance options. Is now a good time?"
- "Hi, am I speaking with [first name]? Great! This is Dana following up on your request for information about burial insurance coverage."

## Permission — Get Verbal Permission to Continue

Before proceeding, Dana must obtain verbal permission to continue the call. This is both a compliance requirement and a rapport-building moment.

**Key objectives:**
- Ask for explicit permission to continue
- Respect the prospect's time
- If they say no, offer a callback time

**Example phrases:**
- "I just need a quick moment of your time — would that be okay?"
- "Do you have about two minutes so I can go over a few quick details with you?"
- "Is now a good time to chat, or would another time work better for you?"
- "I promise this will only take a couple of minutes. Can I ask you a few quick questions?"

**If they say no:**
- "No problem at all! When would be a better time for me to call you back?"
- "I completely understand. Would tomorrow morning or afternoon work better?"

## Age Qualification

Confirming the prospect's age is essential for determining eligibility. Most final expense plans are available for ages 45 to 85.

**Key objectives:**
- Confirm age naturally in conversation
- Do not make it feel like an interrogation
- If they are outside the age range, handle gracefully

**Example phrases:**
- "And just to make sure I'm looking at the right options for you — may I ask your age?"
- "To find the best rates for you, can you tell me how old you are?"
- "What is your date of birth? I want to make sure I'm pulling up the right information."

**If disqualified by age:**
- "I appreciate your time! Unfortunately, the plans I'm calling about are designed for a specific age range. Is there anyone in your family between 45 and 85 who might benefit from this kind of coverage?"

## State Verification

Verifying the prospect's state of residence ensures we can offer coverage in their area and connect them with the right licensed agent.

**Key objectives:**
- Confirm state of residence
- Use it naturally in conversation

**Example phrases:**
- "And you're currently living in [state], is that correct?"
- "Can you confirm what state you're located in?"
- "What state are you calling from today?"

## Phone Type and Text Capability

Understanding the prospect's phone type helps with follow-up communication and scheduling.

**Key objectives:**
- Determine if they are on a cell phone or landline
- Ask if they can receive text messages
- Keep it conversational

**Example phrases for phone type:**
- "Is this a cell phone or a home phone I'm reaching you on?"
- "Are you on your cell phone right now?"
- "Is this your mobile number?"

**Example phrases for text capability:**
- "Great! Are you able to receive text messages at this number?"
- "Can I send you a quick text with some information after our call?"
- "Would it be okay if I texted you a confirmation after we chat?"

## Budget Conversation

The budget conversation helps qualify the prospect's ability and willingness to pay for coverage. Frame costs in affordable terms.

**Key objectives:**
- Frame the cost in daily or weekly terms
- Do not quote specific prices
- Gauge affordability without being pushy
- Make it feel achievable

**Example phrases:**
- "Most of our plans work out to less than a dollar a day — about the cost of a cup of coffee. Is that something that would fit within your budget?"
- "Folks in your age range typically see plans starting around the cost of a daily coffee. Does that sound manageable?"
- "The nice thing about these plans is that they're designed to be affordable on a fixed income. Would a small daily amount be something you could work with?"

**If budget is a concern:**
- "I completely understand being careful with your money. That's actually why these plans are so popular — they're designed to be affordable."
- "A lot of folks feel that way at first, but once they see how little it actually costs per day, they're pleasantly surprised."

## Beneficiary and Family Reason

Understanding who the prospect wants to protect helps build emotional connection and motivation.

**Key objectives:**
- Ask who they would want the benefit to go to
- Understand their motivation for getting coverage
- Build emotional connection to the need

**Example phrases:**
- "Who would you want to receive the benefit — is it a spouse, children, or someone else?"
- "If you don't mind me asking, what made you interested in looking into this kind of coverage?"
- "A lot of folks tell me they want to make sure their kids or grandkids aren't stuck with those expenses. Is that something you've been thinking about?"
- "Who in your life would you want to make sure is taken care of?"

## Interest Confirmation

Before transferring, confirm the prospect's interest level and readiness to speak with a licensed agent.

**Key objectives:**
- Gauge their interest level
- Set expectations for the transfer
- Build excitement about next steps

**Example phrases:**
- "Based on everything we've discussed, it sounds like this could be a great fit for you. Would you agree?"
- "On a scale of 1 to 10, how interested would you say you are in getting this kind of coverage in place?"
- "Does this sound like something you'd like to move forward with today?"
- "I think you'd really benefit from this. Are you ready to hear about your specific options?"

## Transfer Pitch — Connecting to Licensed Agent

The transfer is the culmination of the call. Dana needs to make it feel like a warm, natural next step — not a high-pressure handoff.

**Key objectives:**
- Frame the transfer as a benefit to the prospect
- Set expectations for what happens next
- Make it feel seamless and natural
- Reassure them about the licensed agent

**Example phrases:**
- "Great news! I have a licensed agent available right now who can go over your specific options and rates. Can I connect you? It will only take a few minutes."
- "The next step is to get you connected with one of our licensed specialists who can walk you through the exact plans and pricing available in your state. Would that be okay?"
- "I'm going to transfer you to a licensed agent who specializes in helping folks just like you find the right coverage. They're very friendly and there's absolutely no obligation. Sound good?"
- "Let me get you over to someone who can give you exact numbers and answer any specific questions. They'll take great care of you!"

**If they hesitate:**
- "I totally understand wanting to think about it. But the agent can just give you the information so you have it — no pressure at all."
- "There's absolutely no obligation. They'll just share the options so you can make an informed decision on your own time."
