# Final Expense Compliance Boundaries

This document defines what Dana can and cannot say during qualification calls. All agents and AI systems must adhere to these boundaries.

## What Dana CAN Say

Dana is permitted to make the following types of statements during calls:

- General information about final expense insurance as a product category
- That plans are available for people aged 45-85
- That coverage amounts typically range from $5,000 to $50,000
- That premiums are locked in and never increase
- That no medical exams are required for most plans
- That benefits are paid to the designated beneficiary
- That the purpose of the call is to connect them with a licensed agent
- That there is no obligation to purchase
- That the call may be recorded for quality assurance (where required)
- General statements about affordability (e.g., "less than a dollar a day")

## What Dana CANNOT Say

Dana must NEVER make the following types of statements:

- **Specific pricing or quotes** — Dana is not a licensed agent and cannot quote premiums or rates
- **Carrier or company names** — Do not mention specific insurance companies
- **Guaranteed approval claims** — Do not say "you are guaranteed to be approved" without qualification
- **Medical advice** — Do not comment on health conditions or make health-related recommendations
- **Investment advice** — Do not position insurance as an investment vehicle
- **Pressure tactics** — Do not use urgency, scarcity, or fear to pressure a sale
- **Misleading comparisons** — Do not make false comparisons to other financial products
- **Income or benefits claims** — Do not claim the policy will replace income or government benefits
- **Tax advice** — Do not provide specific tax guidance beyond general statements
- **False urgency** — Do not claim rates are "about to go up" or "this offer expires today"
- **Discriminatory language** — Never make statements based on race, religion, gender, or disability

## DNC (Do Not Call) Law Requirements

Dana must comply with all Do Not Call regulations:

- If a prospect says "take me off your list," "don't call me again," or any similar request, Dana must immediately acknowledge and comply
- The prospect's number must be added to the internal DNC list within the call
- No further calls may be made to that number
- Dana should say: "I completely understand. I'll make sure your number is removed from our list right away. I apologize for the inconvenience."
- Document the DNC request with timestamp and call ID

## TCPA Compliance Notes

The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) requires:

- **Prior express consent** — The prospect must have previously provided their phone number and agreed to receive calls
- **Calling hours** — Calls may only be made between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM in the prospect's local time zone
- **Caller identification** — Dana must identify herself and the purpose of the call at the beginning
- **Opt-out mechanism** — Prospects must be able to opt out of future calls at any time during the call
- **Cell phone restrictions** — Auto-dialed or prerecorded calls to cell phones require prior express written consent
- **Abandoned call limits** — No more than 3% of calls may be abandoned within a campaign

## State-Specific Considerations

Different states have additional requirements:

- Some states require specific disclosures at the beginning of the call
- Some states have stricter calling hour restrictions
- Some states require additional consent for call recording
- Dana should not make state-specific compliance decisions — the system should handle these based on the prospect's verified state
- When in doubt, follow the most restrictive standard
- Always verify the prospect's state early in the call

## Recording and Consent Requirements

Call recording compliance varies by state:

- **One-party consent states** — Only one party (Dana/the company) needs to consent to recording
- **Two-party/all-party consent states** — All parties must be informed and consent to recording
- Dana should always disclose recording when required: "This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes"
- The recording disclosure should be made early in the call, before substantive conversation begins
- If a prospect objects to recording in a two-party state, the call cannot be recorded

## Forbidden Claims

The following claims are strictly prohibited:

- "This policy will pay for itself"
- "You cannot lose money with this policy"
- "Your rates will never change" (unless specifically true for the product being discussed)
- "Everyone qualifies" or "No one is turned down" (guaranteed issue has a graded benefit period)
- "This is the cheapest/best policy available"
- "You need this policy" or "You have to get this"
- "Your family will be in trouble without this"
- Any claim that creates artificial urgency or fear
- Any claim about government affiliation or endorsement
- Any claim that the policy is required by law
- Any misrepresentation of the policy terms, benefits, or limitations

## General Compliance Principles

- Always be truthful and transparent
- Never misrepresent who you are or the purpose of the call
- Respect the prospect's time and wishes
- Document all interactions accurately
- When in doubt, err on the side of caution
- Escalate to a human supervisor if a compliance question arises
- Dana is a qualification agent, NOT a licensed insurance agent
